Exercise is an important component in achieving and maintaining a healthy weight. When you exercise, your body burns calories and uses stored energy, which can lead to weight loss over time. Regular exercise can also increase your metabolism, which means you will burn more calories even when you're not exercising. Exercise also helps build and maintain muscle mass, which is important for weight loss because muscle tissue burns more calories than fat tissue. The more muscle you have, the more calories you burn, even at rest. Exercise also helps improve cardiovascular health and can lower the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and certain types of cancer. It's important to note that exercise alone may not result in significant weight loss, especially if you don't change your diet. However, incorporating regular exercise into a healthy diet and lifestyle can lead to sustainable weight loss and improved overall health.

  • Burbees
    1. Start in a standing position with your feet shoulder-width apart.
    2. Lower yourself into a squat position and place your hands on the floor in front of you.
    3. Kick your feet back into a plank position, keeping your arms extended.
    4. Immediately return your feet to the squat position.
    5. Jump up from the squat position, reaching your arms overhead.
    6. Repeat for 10-15 reps.

  • High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)
    1. Choose any cardio exercise such as running, cycling or jumping jacks.
    2. Perform the exercise at maximum intensity for 30-60 seconds.
    3. Rest for 15-30 seconds.
    4. Repeat for 10-15 minutes.

  • Squat Jumps
    1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart.
    2. Lower yourself into a squat position, keeping your chest up and your knees behind your toes.
    3. Jump up as high as you can, reaching your arms overhead.
    4. Land softly and immediately return to the squat position.
    5. Repeat for 10-15 minutes
